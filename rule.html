Rules & Regulations


1. Code of Conduct:
Bad manners and disrespectful behavior will not be tolerated. Students are expected to maintain a professional and courteous demeanor at all times.

2. Adherence to Office Rules:
All participants are required to follow the rules and regulations set by the institution. Any violation may result in disciplinary action.

3. Timely Fee Payment:
Course fees must be paid on the specified date. A grace period of one day will be allowed; however, failure to submit fees within this timeframe will result in restricted access to classes.

4. Attendance Requirement:
To be eligible for course completion and additional offers, students must maintain an attendance rate greater than 95%. Excessive absenteeism may lead to consequences, including disqualification from certain benefits.

5.Punctuality:
Students are expected to be punctual for all classes. Late entry disrupts the learning environment and will not be tolerated. Habitual tardiness may result in disciplinary measures.

6.Pay After Placement Eligibility:
Students enrolled in the Pay After Placement program must meet specific eligibility criteria, which may include academic performance, project submissions, and other requirements as outlined by the institution.

7.Fee Refund Policy:
Course fees are refundable only within the first 10 days of the course commencement. After this period, no refunds will be processed.

8.Academic Integrity:
Plagiarism, cheating, or any form of academic dishonesty will not be tolerated. Violation of academic integrity may result in immediate dismissal from the course.

9.Communication Protocol:
All official communication from the institution will be conveyed through designated channels. It is the responsibility of the students to regularly check their emails and other communication platforms for updates and announcements.

10.Participation in Evaluations:
Students are required to actively participate in all evaluations, assessments, and examinations as part of the course requirements. Failure to do so may affect academic standing.

11.Maintaining Learning Environment:
Students are expected to contribute positively to the learning environment by respecting fellow students, faculty, and staff. Disruptive behavior may lead to disciplinary action. These rules and regulations are designed to create a conducive and respectful learning environment for all participants. Failure to comply with these rules may result in consequences ranging from warnings to dismissal from the course, depending on the severity of the violation.
